Village of Oak Park

About

In Cook County, Illinois, close to Chicago, there is a community called Oak Park. According to the estimated population from the 2020 U.S. Census, it is the 29th-most populous municipality in Illinois with 54,583 residents. After separating from Cicero, Oak Park was first established in 1835 and later incorporated in 1902. Frank Lloyd Wright, an architect, and his wife moved to Oak Park in 1889. His work had a significant impact on the neighborhood's architecture and design, notably the Frank Lloyd Wright Home and Studio. [6] Railroads and street cars that connected the community to jobs in nearby Chicago over time sped up development. The Open Housing Ordinance, which Oak Park enacted in 1968, assisted in coming up with plans to integrate the community rather than resegregate it.

With 80% or greater voter turnout in each presidential election since 2000, Oak Park is still known for its socially liberal policies today while maintaining its ethnic diversity.

The Green Line and Blue Line "L" train lines of the Chicago Transit Authority, as well as the Metra Oak Park station downtown, provide Oak Park with direct access to Chicago.

Geography

The city of Chicago is located just west of Oak Park. The two municipalities are divided by North Avenue/Illinois Route 64 on the north side of the village and Austin Boulevard on the east side of Oak Park. From Austin to Lombard, Roosevelt Road/Illinois Route 38 forms the southern border between Cicero and Oak Park. From Lombard to Harlem Avenue, Berwyn forms the northern boundary. Its western border is formed by Harlem/Illinois Route 43, which borders Forest Park to the west between North Boulevard and North Avenue and River Forest to the east between Roosevelt and South Boulevard.

The entirety of Oak Park is located on the shore of the historic Lake Chicago, which was the predecessor to today's Lake Michigan and covered the majority of Chicago during the last Ice Age. The lake's shoreline is marked by Ridgeland Avenue in east Oak Park, which was previously a ridge. Prehistoric Oak Park was a "Plains river Delta" system because the ancient Des Plaines river previously spilled into glacial Lake Chicago, similar to how the Chicago River is situated geographically, connecting to the present-day Lake Michigan just north of the city's Loop. Oak Park is located at one of the four continental divisions in North America. The Saint Lawrence River watershed and the Mississippi River watershed are divided by this modest elevation that runs north-south through the hamlet. Two plaques, one on Lake Street near Forest Avenue and the other in the northwest corner of Taylor Park, identify this division..

Oak Park has a total land area of 4.7 sq mi (12.17 km2) as per the 2010 Census..

Transportation

Public transport Five CTA stations in Oak Park provide service on the Green Line and Blue Line, making Oak Park reachable from Chicago. The Union Pacific / West Line of Metra has a stop in Oak Park as well. The CTA and Pace both offer bus transit service both within Oak Park and to surrounding areas.

Addresses, roads, and expressways The main expressway connecting Chicago with Oak Park is the Eisenhower Expressway. O'Hare International Airport can be reached by links from the roadway as well. Oak Park's main east-west roadways continue east into Chicago.

The streets are organized in a grid form, with certain small streets occasionally leading to cul-de-sacs to preserve a sense of place. With no east or west designation, Oak Park's street naming system starts at Austin Boulevard for east-west streets and at the elevated train tracks just south of Lake Street for north-south streets. From there, the numbers divide, getting larger as they go north or south and requiring a north or south designation on addresses. The border streets correspond to the address systems of the cities that share them rather than the Oak Park numbering system. Addresses on Austin Boulevard, for instance, correspond to the Chicago system, with the zero line at Madison Street, and along North Avenue, with Austin Boulevard at 6000 West and Harlem at 7200 West. Furthermore, Elizabeth Court, which is a part of the Frank Lloyd Wright Historic District, still bears the same numbering. There are one to twelve homes on that cul-de-sac.

Bicycles The Illinois Prairie Path's trailhead is less than a mile from Oak Park, and Augusta Boulevard, which runs through the hamlet, is a part of the Grand Illinois Trail. With numerous cycling clubs and organizations, Oak Park is known as a bicycle-friendly town. The community's tree-lined streets and accessibility to trails in neighboring cities draw cyclists to Oak Park, which is accessible by the Green Line, Blue Line, or Metra. Although there are marked bike lanes on several of the roadways in Oak Park, no completely separated cycle facilities are yet in place.

In 2016, the Chicago-based Divvy bike sharing system expanded to Oak Park.

The Divvy service was discontinued by Oak Park's Village Board in 2017 because it was found to be inefficient.

Government



Village

Oak Park has been governed by a council-manager system of local government since 1951. The village board, which is also elected, engages a village manager to handle day-to-day operations on behalf of the elected president and village board. Additionally, Oak Park has five other governments that collect real estate taxes. These comprise Oak Park Township, the River Forest-adjacent River Forest School District, the Oak Park Township, the High School District, the Elementary School District, the Library District, and the Park District.

Both the main Oak Park post office at 901 Lake Street and the Oak Park South post office at 1116 Garfield Street are run by the United States Postal Service.

Elections A two-stage election procedure is used to choose the village board, village president, and other elected officials in Oak Park. Government officials are chosen in general elections, but party nominees are chosen via primaries. Depending on whether it is a local or national election, Oak Park's voter turnout fluctuates substantially. With 79.8% of registered voters casting ballots, Oak Park had the highest voter participation in Cook County's suburbs in the 2012 presidential election. In accordance with state law, municipal elections for the village clerk and board of trustees are held in the spring and often have a substantially lower voter turnout, averaging approximately 20%. Since the national political parties do not nominate candidates for the municipal elections, they are regarded as nonpartisan. Candidates inspire involvement in neighborhood concerns by stepping forward or by being recruited by a citizens group that looks for people to have new candidates for each election cycle.

Schools

Library in Oak Park

The Oak Park Elementary School District runs the public elementary schools (Lincoln, Mann, Longfellow, Beye, Irving, Holmes, Whittier, and Hatch) as well as the middle schools Percy Julian Middle School (previously Nathaniel Hawthorne) and Gwendolyn Brooks Middle School (formerly Ralph Waldo Emerson). The entire municipal limits are served by elementary school District 97, which includes these ten schools.

Periodically, reports on the performance of Oak Park's schools as determined by required statewide exams are made public.

To encourage minority pupils to pursue their education, the junior high schools—now middle schools—were renamed after notable African-Americans rather than well-known American authors. As of the publication date of the report, there is still a performance disparity in schools, which is referred to as "this intolerable and chronic inequity".

There are two high schools in Oak Park: Fenwick High School and Oak Park and River Forest High School, the only schools in educational District 200 that also serve the entire city[31]. While Fenwick High School is a Catholic college preparatory institution operated by the Dominicans and associated with the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Chicago, Oak Park and River Forest High School is a public school with a district that includes both Oak Park and the nearby River Forest. High academic standards have long been a tradition at both high schools. The Tradition of Excellence Award is given by Oak Park and River Forest High School to notable graduates, such as Ernest Hemingway, Ray Kroc, Dan Castellaneta, George Trafton, a member of the football Hall of Fame, Mary Elizabeth Mastrantonio, an actress, astronomer Chad Trujillo, a geochemist named Wally Broecker, and an advocate for the environment named Phil Radford. One of only seven secondary schools in Illinois having the authority to initiate students into the Cum Laude Society is Oak Park and River Forest High School.

District Park

The Oak Park Park District was initially established in 1912 as the Village of Oak Park's Recreation Department. Under Josephine Blackstock's leadership and that of Lilly Ruth Hanson, who took over after her, the organization started an active program of village recreation. Blackstock gave the playgrounds the names of well-known children's authors.

The Recreation Department was abolished in the late 1980s, and the Park District of Oak Park was established as a distinct tax-collecting entity. It consists of two outdoor swimming pools, the Oak Park Conservatory, a historic home that can be rented out for events for a fee, and thirteen parks spread out throughout the hamlet, totaling 80 acres (320,000 m2) of parkland. Additionally, the Park District offers dog exercise facilities where dog owners can bring their animals in exchange for a charge. The 1962-built Ridgeland Common is located at the intersection of Lake Street and Ridgeland Avenue and features a second outdoor pool, an official-sized ice rink, a green roof, and synthetic-turf playing fields. From March 2013 until June 14, 2014, it underwent a comprehensive renovation.

community library

The Oak Park Public Library was established as a public library in 1903 after choosing its first board of trustees. The library contains two branches: the Dole Branch Library and the Maze Branch Library, as well as a main campus that overlooks Scoville Park near the intersection of Oak Park Avenue and Lake Street. The Oak Park Public Library gives its cardholders access to almost 8 million materials as a part of the SWAN library consortium.

Fire

The Oak Park Fire Department currently operates out of three fire stations dispersed around the village: Fire Station #1 (headquarters), Fire Station #2 (north), and Fire Station #3 (south). Each station is overseen by a battalion chief who is in charge of a particular shift. Three ALS engines, one ALS truck, three ALS ambulances, one ALS paramedic squad, one command unit, and many specialist MABAS divisional apparatus are all operated by the Oak Park Fire Department.
